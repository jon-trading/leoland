# Leoland Institutions Posture v1

## Why institutions come before party theatre

Leoland is not trying to teach that governance is mostly scandal, spin, or factional humiliation.

So the first deeper simulation layer is institution-centered.

That means the player encounters the country through:

- schools
- healthcare
- housing
- transport
- local services
- emergency resilience
- community life
- civic information

This is closer to how people actually feel a society.

## What institutions represent

Institutions in Leoland are not abstract stats.
They represent whether ordinary life works well enough for people to feel:

- safe
- respected
- included
- supported
- able to trust the country

## What this prevents

By modeling institutions first, the game avoids drifting too early into:

- minister drama
- party gossip
- cynical gotcha-politics
- cartoon ideology battles

Differences in outlook can still exist later, but they should sit on top of civic life rather than replace it.

## Design consequence

If a future mechanic makes institutions feel secondary to spectacle, it is probably moving away from Leoland's best self.
