# Leoland Visual Identity v1

## Core feeling

Leoland should feel:

- warm
- intelligent
- civic
- hopeful
- slightly playful
- calm but alive

It should not feel:

- harsh
- sterile
- corporate
- dark for the sake of seriousness
- childish in a patronising way

## Visual thesis

The country should look like a place worth caring about.

The UI should feel like a caring civic instrument rather than a political war room. It should suggest stewardship, weather, coastlines, public life, and everyday dignity.

## Color direction

Use a palette that feels like:

- deep ocean blue for national space and calm
- sea-green / teal for life and continuity
- warm sand and parchment for human warmth
- leaf and meadow tones for land and renewal
- gold for guidance, value, and moments of civic emphasis
- coral / rose only for pressure or strain, not as a dominant warning aesthetic

## Map direction

The country map should feel illustrated and curated.

For this phase:

- overhead perspective
- island in open water
- readable regions
- visible coasts and civic centers
- simple but attractive land/ocean contrast

The map is not just decoration. It is the home screen and dashboard surface.

## Typography direction

Typography should feel:

- legible on a phone
- warm and slightly literary in headings
- clean and readable in body copy

A good split for now is:

- serif or soft-display feeling for titles
- clean sans-serif for controls and readable copy

## Icon and UI style

Icons and UI marks should feel:

- soft-edged
- practical
- friendly
- clear at small sizes

Cards, chips, and panels should feel like they belong to the same civic object, not like a collection of random app widgets.

## Emotional art rule

If a screen feels clever but not caring, it is probably moving away from Leoland.

If a screen feels warm, readable, and quietly meaningful, it is probably moving in the right direction.
