# Leoland Game DNA v1

Leoland is a gentle but serious society game.

It is about helping a whole country live well over time.

## What kind of game this is

Leoland is not a conquest game, not a scandal simulator, and not a cynical politics parody. It is a stewardship game.

The player is asked a simple but demanding question:

Can you help a society become happier, healthier, safer, fairer, more prosperous, more trusting, and more resilient over time?

## What good leadership means in Leoland

Good leadership in Leoland means:

- noticing pressure early
- protecting dignity in ordinary life
- balancing regions instead of flattering one bloc
- solving problems before they become theatrical crises
- choosing patience over vanity
- improving public life without hollowing out trust
- treating fairness as a strength, not a weakness

## What common sense means in Leoland

In Leoland, common sense is not code for selfishness or simple-mindedness.

It means:

- practical care
- proportionate decisions
- protecting what matters most first
- refusing false choices when a balanced answer is possible
- understanding that brittle success is not success

## What kind of emotional atmosphere the game should create

The game should feel:

- warm
- thoughtful
- civic
- hopeful
- a little playful
- emotionally safe for younger players
- serious enough that choices still matter

The player should feel responsibility, not dread.

## What must never become the center of the game

These may exist at the edges of the world, but they are not the core loop:

- humiliation politics
- scandal farming
- faction trolling
- domination for its own sake
- cruelty as efficiency
- empty power theatre

## Core test

If a future mechanic makes Leoland feel more cruel, more brittle, or more cynical than wise, fair, and alive, it is moving away from the game DNA and should be reconsidered.
